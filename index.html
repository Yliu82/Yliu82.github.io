About Me

I'm an industrial and UI designer passionate about human-centered technology and design storytelling. I explore the emotional layer of interactive products, and my works span physical installations, companion toys, and adaptive systems that serve marginalized or overlooked groups. As a graduate of Pratt Institute, I bridge empathy and research to create meaningful tools for connection, protection, and play.

My design practice blends fieldwork with prototyping, from AR pet toys for loneliness and ADHD, to emergency curtain systems at bus stops, and speculative biomimicry architecture. I enjoy building interfaces, writing narratives, and testing interaction with real users. Design, for me, is the articulation of care, in both form and experience.

What I'm Doing

UX Research & Data

I conduct contextual inquiries, behavioral mapping, and user testing to inform design decisions with clarity and care.

Product & AR Design

From modular toys to assistive wearables, I develop physical and digital tools that invite interaction, empathy, and joy.

Design Communication

I turn complex insights into clear, visual narratives through storyboarding, layout, and interface mockups.

Prototyping & Fabrication

Using Rhino, Keyshot, 3D printing, and Arduino, I prototype and iterate on form, function, and storytelling.
